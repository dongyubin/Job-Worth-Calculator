The most expensive parts of a job are not always visible in the offer letter. Commute, overtime, and recovery time can quietly consume the life that salary is supposed to support.

## Commute time belongs in the calculation

If a job requires a long commute every day, that time is part of the job's cost. It reduces rest, learning time, family time, and energy for everything outside work.

## Hidden overtime lowers the value of salary

Regular overtime that is not reflected in compensation changes the real deal. A good salary can become mediocre when the actual hours are far above the contract.

## The cost compounds over time

One difficult week may be manageable. A difficult routine repeated for months can affect health, relationships, and career decisions.
